Mass Consumption

An average consumer throws away 31.75 kgs of clothing per year. Globally we produce 13 million tons of textile waste each year 95% of which could be reused or recycled.

Fast Fashion

Fast fashion is a model of mass-producing cheaply made, ‘of-the-moment’ items that are sold at a lower price point. Fast fashion garments spark a lot of ethical concerns.

The Thrift Store

Ditch fast fashion like it’s last week’s trends and start shopping thrift! It is a great way to find high quality, unique garments at a fraction of the price of buying new.

Eco-Fashion

Research and buy from eco-fashion companies who are pursuing the production of clothing with more environmentally friendly methods.

About Us

  • MISSION : Educate the populace about the dire effects of fast fashion by encouraging them to choose more sustainable forward brands.
  • VISION : To enable people to make choices encompassing ethical and sustainably produced products.
  • STRATEGY : Produce and share sustainable methods, set forth the scope of thrift shops and alternatives to fast fashion.

Before we get started on all the different aspects there are to consider as a consumer, we want to address that there are two perspectives: that of brands and that of consumers - YOU!
